CuddleBits

The CuddleBits are little zoomorphic furballs that were used as a platform to answer questions in affective human robot interaction.

An initiative at the Sensory Perception and Interaction lab was to create a "Cuddle Bot", a large, multi degree-of-freedom (DOF) robot pet that responds to affective touch. But designing such a robot is incredibly complex. Paul Bucci decided to decompose the multi-DOF CuddleBot into many simple 1-DOF robots--each which can be rapidly iterated and tested upon. These are the CuddleBits.

I was a collaborating researcher with the CuddleBits, and used them as a research platform for my own studies. Some activities I did:

  • iterated on particular cuddlebit form factors
  • ran user studies
  • conducted interviews
  • contributed to report writing
