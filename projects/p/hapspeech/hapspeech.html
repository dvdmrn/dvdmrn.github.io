Haptic Speech Enhancement

Collaborators: Karon MacLean, Bryan Gick, Sophy Chu, Hanna Elbaggari, Andrew Yang, Tamara Lottering

Can feeling someone's speech through your skin help you understand them better? This project investigated the degree to which haptic feedback can enhance the intelligibility of speech in noise.

In this project, I:

  • Architected the system
  • Supervised a team of undergrad researchers
  • Developed experimental software
  • Conducted quantiative hypothesis testing on experimental data
  • Presented findings and technical report writing

We utilized a single channel voice coil actuator to deliver haptic feedback to participants similar to the concomitant laryngeal vibrations experienced during voiced speech. We manipulated vibration style, actuator placement, phonemic contrast, and synchrony between speech and haptic stimulation. We found a significant 9% enhancement of speech intelligibility when using the device, and that placement and synchrony did not matter.
