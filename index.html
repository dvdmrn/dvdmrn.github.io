I am currently a MSc student in the department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the McGill University Shared Reality Lab. My work focuses on multimodal natural language interfaces between intelligent agents.

I am also a self proclaimed "video generalist". What does that mean? I have expertise in end-to-end video production, encompassing: directing, cinematography, motion graphics, editing, and producing. Top clients of mine include: The Globe and Mail, Coast Capital Savings, Dentons, and many more.

Okay one more "I am" statement I swear: I'm also an illustrator and comic artist. I created the webcomic leftoversalad.

Cognitive Systems unites the fields of linguistics, psychology, philosophy, and computer science to study the design of intelligent systems and the interfaces between them. My emphasis was in Language--it's structure and cognitive realization. Examples of courses taken: cognitive neuroscience, syntax, software engineering, human-computer interaction (HCI). research

My research spans the fields of HCI/HRI, AI and cognitive science. I'm broadly interested in designing for subsymbolic, emergent, or non-compositional aspects of Language. Such techniques can be used to offload the representational work required by an intelligent system, and create engaging user experiences (that may have otherwise been degraded using a system with over-engineered intelligence.)
Some examples of past work includes using your voice as a basis to control an emotionally expressive robot pet, or using vibrations to enhance the intelligibility of speech in noise.

Research Interests
  • Interactive alignment between natural and artificial social agents, specifically within communicative tasks
  • Subsymbolic representation of natural language semantics/pragmatics.
  • Multimodal communication
  • Affective computing
  • HCI/HRI
  • Metaphor
